Crypto-jacking

What is Crypto-jacking?

 Crypto-jacking is when an attacker hijacks a computer or multiple computers, usually through a website, to mine bitcoin. The biggest cost of bitcoin mining is electricity, and cryptojacking allows websites to transfer this cost to the visitor’s CPU.

What does Crypto-jacking do?

 SomeOrdinaryGamers, a YouTube channel, did an experiment to show how a crypto-jacking virus operates. When he allowed the Crypto-jacking makes the compromised computer mine for bitcoin. If there are a group of computers, they will communicate with each other send information to each other. The cryptojacking malware will usually activate while the user is idle

How can I get Crypto-jacked?

 The attacker can create a website that infects visitors with a crypto-jacking bot. Or the attacker can infect an already made site, like a public one that they know will have lots of visitors, and use it to infect . Sometimes the malware will only operate as long as the visitor stay on that site.

 The malware will usually only sap a little bit of power from the victim’s CPU so it is unnoticeable, but if the attacker has hijacked multiple devices then he would easily have enough power to profitably mine bitcoin.

Solution

 Currently, it is hard to detect widespread cryptojacking because of how unnoticeable it can be, but opening up Wireshark and checking can at least help you see if your computer is communicating with foreign devices. The most helpful thing to do right now is spread awareness and avoid installing sketchy apps. Also crypto-jacking most likely is not done by the site owner themselves but someone who has compromised their the owner’s website. If you find your computer to comprised you can try deleting website extensions since that is where most of malware hides.
